What is Geometry?

Geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the sizes, shapes, positions, angles, and dimensions of things. The word "geometry" comes from the Greek words "geo" meaning "earth" and "metron" meaning "measure".

In basic geometry, we learn about points, lines, shapes, and space. Understanding geometry helps us in many real-life situations like building things, playing sports, and even creating art!

Geometry has been studied for thousands of years. Ancient Egyptians used geometry to measure land after the Nile River flooded. Ancient Greeks like Euclid organized geometry into a logical system that we still learn from today!
