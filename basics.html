Geometry Basics

Learn about points, lines, rays, angles, and more!

Point

A point

A point is an exact location in space. It has no size, no length, no width, and no depth. We usually represent a point with a dot and name it with a capital letter.

In geometry, a point is considered a fundamental building block. All other geometric shapes are made up of points. For example, a line is made up of an infinite number of points.

Examples:

  • The tip of a pencil
  • A location on a map
  • The center of a circle

Line

A line extending infinitely in both directions

A line is a straight path that extends infinitely in both directions. It has no thickness and is made up of an infinite number of points. A line is named by any two points on it.

Lines can be classified in different ways: horizontal (left-right), vertical (up-down), or diagonal. Parallel lines never meet, while intersecting lines cross at a point.

Examples:

  • The edge of a ruler
  • A straight road
  • A tight string

Line Segment

A line segment with two endpoints

A line segment is part of a line with two endpoints. Unlike a line, it has a definite length. It is named by its two endpoints.

Line segments are the sides of polygons. For example, a triangle has three line segments, a square has four, and so on. The length of a line segment can be measured with a ruler.

Examples:

  • A piece of straight wire
  • The edge of a book
  • A straw

Ray

A ray with one endpoint extending infinitely

A ray is part of a line that has one endpoint and extends infinitely in one direction. It is named by its endpoint and any other point on the ray.

Rays are important in understanding angles. An angle is formed by two rays that share a common endpoint (called the vertex). Think of a ray as a beam of light coming from a flashlight.

Examples:

  • Sunlight
  • A laser pointer beam
  • The path of an arrow shot from a bow

Angle

An angle formed by two rays

An angle is formed by two rays that share a common endpoint (called the vertex). Angles are measured in degrees (°).

Angles are classified by their measures: acute (less than 90°), right (exactly 90°), obtuse (between 90° and 180°), straight (exactly 180°), and reflex (between 180° and 360°).

Examples:

  • Clock hands at different times
  • Corners of a book (right angles)
  • Open scissors
  • A partially open door
